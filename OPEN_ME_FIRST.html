Section 1 of 3
Enter your options
These let your bot connect to your exchange and trade on your behalf. Find them in your exchange account under API Management or API Settings.
Exchange API Key
Log into your exchange → go to Account → API Management → Create API Key. Copy the key and paste it here.
API Secret
This is shown only once when you create the key. If you lost it, delete the old key and create a new one.
Section 2 of 3
Enter your credentials
Tell the bot where your wallet is and what you want it to trade.
Wallet Address
Your Ethereum wallet address. This is where your funds are held. Starts with 0x for Ethereum-based chains.
Trading Pair
The two assets your bot will trade between. Examples: BTC/USDT, ETH/USDT, BNB/USDT
Risk % Per Trade
What percentage of your balance to use per trade. 1-2% is safe for beginners. Never go above 5%.
Stop Loss %
Bot will automatically sell if your trade drops by this percentage. 5% is a safe starting point.
Take Profit %
Bot will automatically sell when your trade gains this percentage. 15% is a good starting point.
Max Trade Size (USD)
The most your bot will put into a single trade. Start small while testing — $100 to $500.
Max Daily Loss (USD)
If your bot loses this much total in one day, it will stop trading. Recommended: 10-20% of your balance.

Section 3 of 3
Deploy your bot
Follow these steps in order to get your bot running 24/7. Each step has a box — fill it in and click Insert to add your info to the instructions.
1
Create a free Render account
Go to render.com and click Sign Up. Use your Google or GitHub account — it's completely free.
2
Upload your bot files to GitHub
Go to github.com/new, create a private repo named my-trading-bot.

Your GitHub Username
Enter your GitHub username so we can show you the exact repo link.
Your repo will be at: github.com/[your-username]/my-trading-bot

Then drag all the bot files from this ZIP into the GitHub repo upload page.
3
Connect your repo to Render
In Render: click New → Web Service → Connect GitHub → select my-trading-bot.
Set the Start Command to: python main.py
4
Add your API keys in Render
In Render go to Environment tab and add these:

API_KEY[ fill in Section 1 above and click Insert ]
API_SECRET[ fill in Section 1 above and click Insert ]
5
Add your wallet & trading settings in Render
WALLET_ADDRESS[ fill in Section 2 above and click Insert ]
TRADING_PAIR[ fill in Section 2 above and click Insert ]
RISK_PCT[ fill in Section 2 above and click Insert ]
STOP_LOSS_PCT[ fill in Section 2 above and click Insert ]
TAKE_PROFIT_PCT[ fill in Section 2 above and click Insert ]
MAX_POSITION_USD[ fill in Section 2 above and click Insert ]
MAX_DAILY_LOSS[ fill in Section 2 above and click Insert ]
6
Add Telegram credentials in Render
Go to Environment Variables in Render and add:
TG_BOT_TOKEN = [ paste your token — click Insert above ]
TG_CHAT_ID = [ paste your chat ID — click Insert above ]
7
Click Deploy — your bot is live!
Click the Create Web Service button in Render. Your bot will start in about 60 seconds and run 24/7 automatically.

You can watch it working in the Logs tab in Render.
Final step
Export your config file
After filling in all the boxes above and clicking Insert, export your config.json file. Add it to your GitHub repo before deploying.
